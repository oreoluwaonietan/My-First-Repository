Coconut Rice Recipe

A simple and fragrant coconut rice can be prepared in one pot using canned coconut milk, water, rice, and salt, with optional sugar for a hint of sweetness. The key to fluffy, not mushy, rice is proper rinsing and a resting period after cooking.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups jasmine rice (or long-grain white rice)
  • 1 (14-ounce) can full-fat coconut milk
  • ¾ cup water (or coconut water for a more intense flavor)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon granulated sugar (optional, for a subtle sweetness)
  • Optional garnishes: toasted shredded coconut, fresh chopped cilantro, or a squeeze of lime juice

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice: Place the rice in a fine-mesh sieve and rinse under cold, running water until the water runs mostly clear. This removes excess starch and prevents the rice from becoming gluey. Drain well.
  2. Combine ingredients: In a medium saucepan, whisk together the coconut milk, water, salt, and optional sugar. Stir in the rinsed rice.
  3. Bring to a boil: Place the saucepan over medium-high heat and bring the mixture to a boil, stirring occasionally to prevent the rice from sticking.
  4. Simmer:Once boiling, immediately reduce the heat to low and cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. Simmer gently, without stirring or lifting the lid, for about 15 minutes, or until the liquid has been absorbed.
  5. Rest and fluff: Remove the pot from the heat and let it sit, covered, for an additional 10 minutes to steam. This step is crucial for an even, fluffy texture.
  6. Serve: Uncover the pot and fluff the rice with a fork. Stir in any optional garnishes like cilantro or a squeeze of lime juice, then serve warm.

Pro Tips for Perfect Results

  1. Use full-fat canned coconut milk for the best flavor and creamy texture; carton coconut milk is often too watered down.
  2. Do not stir the rice while it simmers to avoid activating the starches and making it sticky (like risotto).
  3. For different flavor profiles, try adding a knotted pandan leaf or kaffir lime leaves to the pot for an authentic Asian-style dish, or curry powder for an Indian twist.
